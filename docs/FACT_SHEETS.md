## Alphabet Inc.

- **Fiscal Year End:** December 31, 2025 (FY2025). The 10-K is for the fiscal year ended December 31, 2025; audit opinion dated February 4, 2026 (page 47).
- **Total Revenue:** $402,836 million (~$402.8 billion) for FY2025, up from $350,018M in 2024 and $307,394M in 2023 (Consolidated Statements of Income, page 50).
- **Net Income:** $132,170 million (~$132.2 billion) for FY2025, up from $100,118M in 2024 and $73,795M in 2023; diluted EPS $10.81 (Consolidated Statements of Income, page 50).
- **Cash And Equivalents Current:** $30,708 million as of December 31, 2025 (Consolidated Balance Sheets, page 49; also confirmed on the Consolidated Statements of Cash Flows "cash and cash equivalents at end of period," page 53). Note: total cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities was $126,843M (page 49).
- **Cash And Equivalents Prior:** $23,466 million as of December 31, 2024 (Consolidated Balance Sheets, page 49; consistent with cash flow statement, page 53).
- **Main Revenue Source:** Google Services is the dominant revenue source: $342,721M of $402,836M total (~85%) in FY2025. Within Google Services, Google advertising is the core driver at $294,691M (Google Search & other $224,532M, YouTube ads $40,367M, Google Network $29,792M). The filing states more than 70% of total revenues came from online advertising in 2025 (page 16 / Note 2 disaggregated revenues, page 61).
- **Liquidity Notes:** Working capital strengthened year over year. Total current assets rose to $206,038M (Dec 31, 2025) from $163,711M (Dec 31, 2024). Total current liabilities rose to $102,745M from $89,122M. Working capital therefore increased to ~$103,293M (206,038 - 102,745) in 2025 from ~$74,589M (163,711 - 89,122) in 2024, an improving trend. Current ratio ~2.01x (2025) vs ~1.84x (2024). All figures from the Consolidated Balance Sheets, page 49. MD&A states Alphabet had $126.8 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term marketable securities at Dec 31, 2025, and expects existing cash, short-term marketable securities, and operating/financing cash flows to be sufficient to fund operations and commitments for at least the next 12 months and thereafter for the foreseeable future (pages 37-38). Operating cash flow was $164,713M in 2025 vs $125,299M in 2024 (page 38).
- **China India Cloud Risk:** Not disclosed in any China- or cloud-specific form. The filing contains ZERO mentions of "China" or "Chinese" anywhere in the document. "India" appears exactly once (page 17), and that single reference is unrelated to cloud services: it lists India among countries ("Brazil, India, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, and Vietnam") that have enacted or are considering AI-focused regulations. There is no country-specific risk factor naming China or India for Google Cloud or any other business. International risk is addressed only generically: the filing notes international revenues were ~52% of consolidated revenues in 2025 and flags broad cross-border risks such as foreign-ownership restrictions, foreign exchange controls limiting cash repatriation, sanctions, tariffs, import/export controls, market-access barriers, political unrest, geopolitical tensions, and armed conflict (specifically citing the Middle East and Ukraine, not China or India) that could impede operating in certain jurisdictions (pages 13-14, international operations risk factor). For Google Cloud specifically, the risks discussed are competitive, execution, public-sector/government customer, and security related, with no China or India geographic call-out.

**Segments:**

  - *Google Services* — $342,721M (FY2025); $304,930M (2024). Operating income $139,404M (2025). Includes Google advertising $294,691M (Google Search & other $224,532M, YouTube ads $40,367M, Google Network $29,792M) plus Google subscriptions, platforms, and devices $48,030M. (p.88 (Note 15); revenue-by-type detail on page 61 (Note 2))
  - *Google Cloud* — $58,705M (FY2025); $43,229M (2024). Operating income $13,910M (2025). Comprises Google Cloud Platform and Google Workspace. (p.88 (Note 15))
  - *Other Bets* — $1,537M (FY2025); $1,648M (2024). Operating loss $(7,515)M (2025). Includes Waymo and other early-stage businesses. (p.88 (Note 15))
  - *Hedging gains (losses) / Alphabet-level (not a segment)* — Revenue-related hedging gains (losses) of $(127)M in 2025 are not allocated to segments. Unallocated Alphabet-level activities (mainly shared AI R&D) carried operating loss of $(16,760)M in 2025. (p.88 (Note 15))


## Amazon.com, Inc.

- **Fiscal Year End:** December 31, 2025 (fiscal year ended December 31, 2025). Source: cover page (page 1) and Consolidated Balance Sheets dated December 31, 2025 (page 63).
- **Total Revenue:** $716,924 million total net sales (FY2025). Source: page 61.
- **Net Income:** $77,670 million for FY2025 (vs. $59,248M in 2024 and $30,425M in 2023). Diluted EPS $7.17. Source: Consolidated Statements of Operations, page 61.
- **Cash And Equivalents Current:** $86,810 million at December 31, 2025 (end of latest fiscal year). Source: Consolidated Balance Sheets, page 63.
- **Cash And Equivalents Prior:** $78,779 million at December 31, 2024 (prior fiscal year-end). Source: Consolidated Balance Sheets, page 63.
- **Main Revenue Source:** By segment net sales, North America is the largest revenue source at $426,305M in 2025 (International $161,894M; AWS $128,725M). By operating income, AWS is the largest contributor at $45,606M of the $79,975M consolidated operating income, while North America contributed $29,619M and International $4,750M. By disaggregated product/service category, "Online stores" is the single largest line at $269,287M in 2025. Source: Note 10 Segment Information, page 109; net sales by category, page 111.
- **Liquidity Notes:** Total current assets: $229,083M (2025) vs. $190,867M (2024). Total current liabilities: $218,005M (2025) vs. $179,431M (2024). Source: Consolidated Balance Sheets, page 63. Working capital (current assets minus current liabilities) is positive but roughly flat to slightly lower year over year: approximately $11,078M in 2025 vs. approximately $11,436M in 2024, a decline of about $358M. Important caveat: the filing does NOT state a labeled "working capital" figure or describe its trend numerically; those figures are computed from the balance sheet. MD&A (pages 39-41) discusses working capital only qualitatively, noting that operating cash flow rose to $139.5B in 2025 (from $115.9B in 2024) driven by higher net income excluding non-cash expenses and changes in working capital, and that working capital at any point is subject to demand, inventory management, payment timing, customer/vendor terms, and FX. Broader liquidity: cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities at fair value were $123.0B (2025) vs. $101.2B (2024); page 39. No revolving credit or commercial paper borrowings outstanding at December 31, 2025; page 41.
- **China India Cloud Risk:** Disclosed under the risk factor "Our International Operations Expose Us to a Number of Risks" (heading on page 11), with the China/India-specific paragraph spanning pages 12-13. Key points: The People's Republic of China (PRC) and India regulate Amazon's and its affiliates' businesses through regulations and license requirements that may restrict (i) foreign investment in and operation of the internet, IT infrastructure, data centers, retail, delivery, and other sectors, (ii) internet content, and (iii) the sale of media and other products/services (page 12). On cloud specifically: to meet local ownership, regulatory licensing, and cybersecurity requirements, Amazon provides "certain technology services in China through contractual relationships with third parties that hold PRC licenses to provide services" (page 13). In India, the government restricts foreign ownership/control of Indian companies engaged in online multi-brand retail trading; for www.amazon.in, Amazon provides marketing tools and logistics services to third-party sellers and holds only an indirect minority interest in an entity that is a third-party seller on the marketplace (page 13). Amazon states there are "substantial uncertainties regarding the interpretation of PRC and Indian laws and regulations," that these governments could take a contrary view, and that its Chinese and Indian businesses may be unable to continue operating if it cannot access sufficient funding or, in China, enforce its contractual relationships. Violations or changed interpretations could result in fines/financial penalties, revoked licenses, forced restructuring, or being forced to shut down entirely (page 13). Additionally, because China-based sellers account for significant portions of third-party seller services and advertising revenues, and China-based suppliers provide significant portions of components and finished goods, regulatory/trade restrictions, tariff policy changes and trade disputes, data-protection and cybersecurity laws, economic factors, geopolitical events, or security issues affecting China-based sellers and suppliers could adversely affect operating results (pages 12-13). Note: the filing frames cloud/data-center risk primarily around China (licensing via third parties); India risk is framed around online retail foreign-ownership restrictions rather than cloud.

**Segments:**

  - *North America* — Net sales $426,305M (2025); $387,497M (2024); $352,828M (2023). Operating income $29,619M (2025). Largest segment by revenue. (p.109)
  - *International* — Net sales $161,894M (2025); $142,906M (2024); $131,200M (2023). Operating income $4,750M (2025). (p.109)
  - *AWS (Amazon Web Services)* — Net sales $128,725M (2025); $107,556M (2024); $90,757M (2023). Operating income $45,606M (2025); largest contributor to consolidated operating income. (p.109)


## Microsoft Corporation

- **Fiscal Year End:** June 30, 2025 (FY2025 / fiscal year ended June 30, 2025). Cover page p.1; income statement p.50.
- **Total Revenue:** $281,724 million (FY2025), up 15% from $245,122M (FY2024) and $211,915M (FY2023). Split: Product $63,946M, Service and other $217,778M. Source: Income Statements, filing p.50 (PDF p.87); also Note 18, p.85.
- **Net Income:** $101,832 million (FY2025), up 16% from $88,136M (FY2024); $72,361M (FY2023). Diluted EPS $13.64. Source: Income Statements, filing p.50 (PDF p.87); MD&A p.36 (line ~2108).
- **Cash And Equivalents Current:** $30,242 million as of June 30, 2025. Source: Balance Sheets, filing p.52 (PDF p.89); also Cash Flow Statements "end of period," filing p.54 (PDF p.91).
- **Cash And Equivalents Prior:** $18,315 million as of June 30, 2024. Source: Balance Sheets, filing p.52 (PDF p.89); Cash Flow Statements, filing p.54 (PDF p.91). (Note: total cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments were $94,565M at FY2025 and $75,543M at FY2024.)
- **Main Revenue Source:** Largest reportable segment by revenue is Productivity and Business Processes at $120,810M (FY2025). On a single product/service-line basis, "Server products and cloud services" (includes Azure) is the largest at $98,435M (FY2025), and the cross-segment "Microsoft Cloud" aggregate revenue was $168.9 billion, up 23%. Within Intelligent Cloud, Azure and other cloud services revenue grew 34%. Sources: segment table filing p.84 (PDF p.143); product-revenue and Microsoft Cloud detail filing p.85 (PDF p.144); MD&A highlights filing p.35 (PDF p.61).
- **Liquidity Notes:** Total current assets: $191,131M (FY2025) vs $159,734M (FY2024). Total current liabilities: $141,218M (FY2025) vs $125,286M (FY2024). Working capital (current assets minus current liabilities) = $49,913M FY2025 vs $34,448M FY2024, an increase of about $15.5 billion year over year (computed; the filing does not state a "working capital" figure or current ratio explicitly). Current ratio improved to ~1.35 (2025) from ~1.28 (2024). The filing's liquidity discussion (MD&A, filing p.41, PDF p.72) states cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments totaled $94.6 billion (FY2025) vs $75.5 billion (FY2024). All balance-sheet figures: Balance Sheets, filing p.52 (PDF p.89).
- **China India Cloud Risk:** The 10-K does NOT contain a China- or India-specific cloud business-risk disclosure. China and India are mentioned only twice by name: (1) China is listed among jurisdictions (EU, UK, U.S., China) actively enforcing/enacting competition and digital-market regulations that could result in fines or hinder Microsoft's ability to provide its software (Risk Factors, filing p.25, PDF p.43); (2) China and India are named as locations of Microsoft's largest owned/leased international properties, including datacenters and R&D facilities (Properties / Item 2, filing p.40, PDF p.58). Cloud-relevant geopolitical/operational risks are discussed generically, not by country: U.S. export controls, the (rescinded) AI Diffusion Rule and potential successor AI rules, restrictions on data flows and outbound investment that "may limit our ability to leverage parts of our global engineering footprint to provide services in certain jurisdictions," tariffs affecting "cloud and devices supply chain cost competitiveness," and accelerating "sovereignty initiatives" among international customers (Risk Factors, filing p.27, PDF p.45). The "global nature of our business" risk factor flags emerging-market/protectionist/nationalist risks (data/local-sourcing restrictions, investment restrictions) without naming China or India (filing p.34, PDF p.51-52). Net: no disclosed standalone China/India cloud risk; only general international/trade/AI-export-control risk language.

**Segments:**

  - *Productivity and Business Processes (largest segment / main revenue source)* — $120,810M FY2025 (vs $106,820M FY2024, +13%); operating income $69,773M. Includes Microsoft 365 Commercial & Consumer, LinkedIn, Dynamics. (p.Filing p.38 (PDF p.68) MD&A; p.84 (PDF p.143) Note 18)
  - *Intelligent Cloud* — $106,265M FY2025 (vs $87,464M FY2024, +21%); operating income $44,589M. Includes Azure & other cloud services (Azure grew 34%), server products, enterprise & partner services. (p.Filing p.38 (PDF p.68) MD&A; p.84 (PDF p.143) Note 18)
  - *More Personal Computing* — $54,649M FY2025 (vs $50,838M FY2024, +7%); operating income $14,166M. Includes Windows & Devices, Gaming (incl. Activision Blizzard / Xbox), Search & news advertising (Bing/Copilot). (p.Filing p.38 (PDF p.68) MD&A; p.84 (PDF p.143) Note 18)


## Apple Inc.

- **Fiscal Year End:** September 27, 2025 (fiscal year 2025; 52 weeks). Cover page references "For the fiscal year ended September 27, 2025" (PAGE 2). Prior fiscal years referenced: September 28, 2024 and September 30, 2023.
- **Total Revenue:** $416,161 million for FY2025 (PAGE 25, PAGE 32).
- **Net Income:** $112,010 million (~$112.0 billion) for FY2025, up from $93,736M in FY2024 and $96,995M in FY2023 (PAGE 32).
- **Cash And Equivalents Current:** $35,934 million as of September 27, 2025 (end of latest fiscal year) — Consolidated Balance Sheets (PAGE 34). Note: separate from marketable securities of $18,763M current and $77,723M non-current.
- **Cash And Equivalents Prior:** $29,943 million as of September 28, 2024 (prior fiscal year end) — Consolidated Balance Sheets (PAGE 34).
- **Main Revenue Source:** By product/service category, iPhone is the single largest revenue source at $209,586M in FY2025 (~50% of total net sales), ahead of Services at $109,158M (PAGE 26). By reportable (geographic) segment, the Americas is the largest at $178,353M in FY2025 (PAGE 25).
- **Liquidity Notes:** Apple operates with NEGATIVE working capital, which narrowed (improved) year over year. FY2025: total current assets $147,957M vs total current liabilities $165,631M, giving working capital of approximately −$17,674M. FY2024: total current assets $152,987M vs total current liabilities $176,392M, giving working capital of approximately −$23,405M. So the working capital deficit shrank by about $5.7 billion versus the prior year, driven by current liabilities falling (−$10,761M, mainly lower other current liabilities) more than current assets fell (−$5,030M). All figures from Consolidated Balance Sheets (PAGE 34). The negative working capital figure is computed from disclosed totals; the filing does not state a "working capital" line directly. Liquidity is supported by large non-current marketable securities ($77,723M, PAGE 34) and strong operating cash flow.
- **China India Cloud Risk:** The 10-K does NOT contain a risk factor that specifically ties cloud-services operations to China or India (e.g., there is no disclosure that iCloud is operated by a local partner in China, and no China/India-specific cloud-hosting risk). Cloud and China/India exposure appear only in broader terms: (1) Cloud services are described generically as storing and syncing customer content across Apple and Windows devices (PAGE 5); there is no country-specific operating risk attached to them. (2) Manufacturing concentration: "A significant majority of the Company's manufacturing is performed... by outsourcing partners located primarily in China mainland, India, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan and Vietnam," with single-source partners and final assembly of substantially all hardware concentrated in Asia (PAGE 11; also PAGE 9 and PAGE 51). (3) Geopolitical/trade risk: Beginning Q2 FY2025 new U.S. tariffs were announced on imports including from China and India, with reciprocal/retaliatory measures and a Section 232 semiconductor investigation; restrictions "can be announced with little or no advance notice" and escalation could cause significant supply-chain disruption (PAGE 9). (4) Greater China segment revenue declined 4% in FY2025 (and 8% in FY2024) mainly on lower iPhone sales (PAGE 25). (5) Regulatory risk: global operations are subject to changing laws on "privacy, data security and data localization," online safety, digital platforms, and national security, which can limit the Company's ability to offer a product, service or feature; this is framed worldwide, not China/India-specific (PAGE 12). In segment structure, India is included within the Europe segment, not Greater China (PAGE 5). Bottom line: no disclosed cloud-specific China/India risk; the relevant risks are manufacturing concentration, tariffs/geopolitics, and generic data-localization regulation.

**Segments:**

  - *Americas (largest segment; North and South America)* — $178,353M FY2025, up 7% (FY2024 $167,045M; FY2023 $162,560M) (p.PAGE 25)
  - *Europe (European countries plus India, Middle East and Africa)* — $111,032M FY2025, up 10% (FY2024 $101,328M; FY2023 $94,294M) (p.PAGE 25)
  - *Greater China (China mainland, Hong Kong, Taiwan)* — $64,377M FY2025, down 4% (FY2024 $66,952M; FY2023 $72,559M) (p.PAGE 25)
  - *Japan* — $28,703M FY2025, up 15% (FY2024 $25,052M; FY2023 $24,257M) (p.PAGE 25)
  - *Rest of Asia Pacific (Australia, New Zealand, other Asian countries not in other segments)* — $33,696M FY2025, up 10% (FY2024 $30,658M; FY2023 $29,615M) (p.PAGE 25)


## Tesla, Inc.

- **Fiscal Year End:** December 31, 2025 (Form 10-K for the fiscal year ended December 31, 2025; auditor's report dated January 28, 2026). Cover page, PDF p.1 (printed cover); auditor report PDF p.89.
- **Total Revenue:** $94,827 million (~$94.83 billion) for FY2025. Source: Consolidated Statements of Operations, printed p.50 (PDF p.92).
- **Net Income:** $3,855 million total net income for FY2025; $3,794 million attributable to common stockholders. Diluted EPS $1.08. Source: Consolidated Statements of Operations, printed p.50 (PDF p.92).
- **Cash And Equivalents Current:** $16,513 million as of December 31, 2025. Source: Consolidated Balance Sheets, printed p.49 (PDF p.90); also cross-referenced in segment/geographic note at PDF p.117 line.
- **Cash And Equivalents Prior:** $16,139 million as of December 31, 2024. Source: Consolidated Balance Sheets, printed p.49 (PDF p.90). (Note: cash and cash equivalents AND restricted cash at end of 2025 was $17,616M and at end of 2024 was $17,037M per the Statement of Cash Flows, PDF p.94.)
- **Main Revenue Source:** The Automotive segment (which includes "automotive & services and other") is the main revenue source, generating $82,056 million of FY2025 revenue versus $12,771 million for the Energy generation and storage segment. Within automotive, Automotive sales (vehicle sales) is the single largest line at $65,821 million. Source: Segment Note 16, printed p.93 (PDF p.169); revenue lines on Statement of Operations, printed p.50 (PDF p.92).
- **Liquidity Notes:** Tesla's liquidity strengthened versus the prior year. Total current assets rose to $68,642M at 12/31/2025 from $58,360M at 12/31/2024. Total current liabilities rose to $31,714M from $28,821M. Working capital (current assets minus current liabilities) was therefore $36,928M at 12/31/2025 versus $29,539M at 12/31/2024, an increase of roughly $7,389M (positive/improving working-capital trend YoY). The current-asset increase was driven largely by short-term investments ($27,546M vs $20,424M), cash and equivalents ($16,513M vs $16,139M), and prepaid/other current assets ($7,615M vs $5,362M). All figures from Consolidated Balance Sheets, printed p.49 (PDF p.90). The current ratio improved to approximately 2.16x (2025) from 2.03x (2024). Note: working capital is computed by the analyst from disclosed balance-sheet totals; the filing does not present a single "working capital" line item.
- **China India Cloud Risk:** INDIA: The filing contains NO references to India at all (zero mentions); there is no India-specific or India-cloud disclosure. CLOUD SERVICES: Tesla does not have a "cloud services" business or segment, and the filing does not discuss cloud-service risks (e.g., AWS/Azure/Google Cloud, SaaS, cloud computing) in China or India. The grep for cloud-computing/cloud-services/public-cloud terms returned no matches. The closest related items are: (1) Tesla's own "AI infrastructure" which "includes our owned data centers" (Note 2, PDF p.137/printed near p.66-72) and data-center/compute investment risk in MD&A (PDF pp.69-70). CHINA (general business/operational risk, primarily privacy/cybersecurity/data-localization and trade): (a) Risk factor on data privacy/cybersecurity states "additional privacy and cybersecurity laws have come into effect in the Asia-Pacific region, including China, and other jurisdictions where Tesla has a market presence," and that Tesla "may also be subject to specific vehicle manufacturer obligations relating to cybersecurity, data privacy and data localization requirements which place additional risks to our international operations" (printed p.51, PDF pp.51-52). (b) Operating-jurisdiction risk factor: Tesla is "a company based in the U.S., with additional manufacturing operations in China and Europe," creating risks around regulatory/safety conformance, trade restrictions, customs, tariffs, exchange controls, and "preferences in foreign nations for domestically manufactured products"; it cannot predict changes to international trade agreements "such as those with China" (PDF pp.41-42, around line 920-944). (c) Tesla operates Gigafactory Shanghai and Megafactory Shanghai in China (Properties, PDF p.61). (d) China Working Capital Facility: an unsecured revolving facility of up to RMB 20.00 billion entered in 2024, amended Mar 2025 (availability extended through Apr 2028) and Sept 2025 (lender commitment increased); interest at Loan Prime Rate of Bank of China minus 0.89% or 0.99% (Note 12, Debt, PDF pp.146-147). (e) China is Tesla's second-largest geographic market by revenue: $20,962M in 2025 (2024: $20,944M; 2023: $21,745M) per geographic revenue table, printed p.93 (PDF p.169). SUMMARY: The filing discloses China business/operational risks (privacy/cybersecurity/data-localization, trade/tariff, manufacturing concentration) but says nothing about India and nothing about cloud-service risks in either country.

**Segments:**

  - *Automotive (includes automotive sales, regulatory credits, leasing, and Services & other)* — $82,056M FY2025 (2024: $87,604M; 2023: $90,738M); segment gross profit $13,292M. This is the main/largest segment. (p.Segment Note 16, printed p.93 (PDF p.169); revenue lines also on Statement of Operations printed p.50 (PDF p.92))
  - *Energy generation and storage* — $12,771M FY2025 (2024: $10,086M; 2023: $6,035M), up 27% YoY; segment gross profit $3,802M. (p.Segment Note 16, printed p.93 (PDF p.169); MD&A printed p.37 (PDF p.74))
